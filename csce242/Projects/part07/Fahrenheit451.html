Fahrenheit 451

by Ray Bradbury

Overview

Fahrenheit 451 is a dystopian novel by Ray Bradbury, first published in 1953. The novel is set in a future society where books are banned, and firemen burn any that are found. It follows Guy Montag, a fireman who begins to question the purpose of his job and the society around him. The book explores themes of censorship, conformity, and the role of knowledge in a free society.

Main Characters

  • Guy Montag: The protagonist, a fireman who begins to question his role in society.
  • Mildred Montag: Montag's wife, who is obsessed with television and disinterested in books.
  • Captain Beatty: Montag's boss, the fire chief who strongly supports the burning of books.
  • Clarisse McClellan: A young woman who opens Montag's eyes to the world around him.
  • Professor Faber: A retired English professor who helps Montag in his quest for knowledge.

Fun Fact

Ray Bradbury wrote the first draft of Fahrenheit 451 in the basement of UCLA's Powell Library on a typewriter that he rented for 10 cents per half hour.
