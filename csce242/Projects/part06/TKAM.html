To Kill a Mockingbird

by Harper Lee

Overview

To Kill a Mockingbird is a novel by Harper Lee published in 1960. The novel is widely regarded as an American classic and deals with serious issues such as racial injustice and the destruction of innocence. Set in the 1930s in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, the story is narrated by Scout Finch, a young girl whose father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer defending a black man accused of raping a white woman.
