Smoke & Mirrors

by Neil Gaiman

Overview

Smoke & Mirrors is a collection of short stories and poems by Neil Gaiman, first published in 1998. The stories span a wide range of genres, from horror to fantasy to science fiction, with themes of love, loss, and the supernatural. Gaimanâ€™s unique storytelling and ability to weave together the bizarre and the familiar make this collection a must-read for fans of speculative fiction.

Main Characters

  • Various Characters: Each story features its own unique characters, exploring different themes and genres.
  • The White Road: A reimagining of a traditional English folk tale.
  • Chivalry: A story where a woman buys the Holy Grail from a second-hand shop.

Fun Fact

Neil Gaiman's *Smoke & Mirrors* includes a short story titled "Shoggoth's Old Peculiar," which is a humorous homage to the works of H.P. Lovecraft.
