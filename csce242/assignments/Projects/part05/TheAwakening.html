The Awakening

by Kate Chopin

Overview

*The Awakening* is a novel by Kate Chopin, first published in 1899. It is set in New Orleans and the Louisiana Gulf coast at the end of the 19th century. The novel tells the story of Edna Pontellier, a woman who questions her conventional role as wife and mother after an emotional awakening.

Main Characters

  • Edna Pontellier: The protagonist who experiences a personal awakening.
  • Léonce Pontellier: Edna's husband, a successful businessman.
  • Robert Lebrun: Edna's love interest.
  • Mademoiselle Reisz: A pianist who influences Edna's awakening.
  • Adèle Ratignolle: Edna's friend who embodies the traditional woman.

Fun Fact

When it was first published, *The Awakening* was controversial for its portrayal of female independence and self-discovery.
