To Kill a Mockingbird

by Harper Lee

Overview

*To Kill a Mockingbird* is a novel by Harper Lee published in 1960. Instantly successful, it became a classic of modern American literature. The novel is renowned for its warmth and humor, despite dealing with serious issues of rape and racial inequality. The story is told through the eyes of Scout Finch as her father, Atticus Finch, defends a black man, Tom Robinson, accused of raping a white woman.

Main Characters

  • Scout Finch: The young narrator of the story.
  • Atticus Finch: Scout's father, a lawyer, and moral hero.
  • Jem Finch: Scout's older brother.
  • Tom Robinson: The black man accused of raping Mayella Ewell.
  • Mayella Ewell: The woman who accuses Tom Robinson of rape.
  • Boo Radley: A reclusive neighbor who becomes a subject of fascination for Scout and Jem.

Fun Fact

Harper Lee based the character of Atticus Finch on her own father, who was also a lawyer and once defended two black men accused of murder.
