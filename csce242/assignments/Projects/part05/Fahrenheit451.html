Fahrenheit 451

by Ray Bradbury

Overview

*Fahrenheit 451* is a dystopian novel by Ray Bradbury published in 1953. The novel is set in a future society where books are banned, and "firemen" burn any that are found. It explores themes of censorship, the power of knowledge, and the dangers of a society where critical thinking is suppressed. The protagonist, Guy Montag, begins to question the purpose of his role as a fireman and seeks out books in defiance of the oppressive system.

Fun Fact

Ray Bradbury originally wrote *Fahrenheit 451* on a typewriter in the basement of a library, renting it for 10 cents per half hour!
